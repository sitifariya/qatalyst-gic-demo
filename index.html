Method and data

Everything here is reproducible from public data.

Problem. Site 8 × 100 MW batteries on the IEEE 118-bus system (pandapower case118) absorbing a 400 MW AI data centre at bus 23, to minimise worst-scenario residual load-not-served under N-1 contingencies. DC power flow with PTDF sensitivities.

Weather. NSRDB PSM3 hourly temperature, Columbus Ohio 2022 (the system derives from the AEP Midwest network), mapped to load multipliers by a stated elasticity. The design event is the actual worst recorded day, Winter Storm Elliott at −9.6 °C, multiplier 1.235.

Rules. Identical wall-clock budgets, one shared scorer for every method, stochastic methods reported over repeated runs, certified optima from a HiGHS MILP, both solver time and wall clock recorded. Cloud upload and queue overhead is reported, not hidden.

Corrections made. An early 45% hybrid win did not reproduce and was withdrawn. An unphysical objective was rebuilt. Unequal budgets were replaced by an equal-time harness. A "best known" claim was withdrawn when the exact solver certified the optimum. All are disclosed in the report.

Geography is illustrative. IEEE 118 has no real coordinates; the layout is force-directed over Ohio. Topology, loads and every result are real.
